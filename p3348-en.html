My Cosmogyny

Because, as we all know, everything rots, rusts, breaks down, into simpler and simpler units. Every day brings us closer to death. Daddy called it “The Second Law of Thermodynamics: You can’t shovel shit into a horse and get hay.”

But, ‘in the beginning’, or sometime along there, a few little bits and pieces decided to get more complicated, against all the odds. Why? Is this the God(dess) principle? So in the primeval soup, some molecules got together and after a long time we had something that was so complicated that it figured out how to reproduce itself (was that viruses? DNA?) and then there was a kind of borderline situation and then something which we could call Life started happening, only in a small way at first and then it got bigger and more complicated and you don’t really want any more detail ….. (some of it is very, very messy) and after a very long time and a lot of false starts and dead ends, living things began communicating (they had to, because by this time Life had developed ways of reproducing itself – that’s the main thing about Life, it seems to want to survive, and it seems to want to continue the genetic line (all except middle-class French women, apparently, they have to be bribed) so the male and the female have to have ways of making decisions about each other to do with reproducing. And then the big brains and small teeth came along, and some of Life had to get together in groups to survive, and then there was the lengthy maturation period, the protracted helplessness that made the pair and the group even more necessary, and we began talking to each other, and forming concepts, and doing some learning instead of operating entirely by instinct (this is much more limited than we would like to believe) (the ‘not operating entirely by instinct’, that is). Then we started having Rules about mating, and a lot of the Rules began to be about males making sure the offspring were their own and not someone else’s, and the Rules were said to have been made up by God, who was of course one of the boys. At some point along the way, the Rules began to insist that the females were dangerous and evil, and what was most dangerous and evil about them was their sexual desire. If you don’t believe this, read Revelations, with particular reference to The Whore of Babylon – look deeply into other religions, seek out their definition of the Root Of All Evil. Buried in among all the pretty language, you will find out that it is female sexual desire.

And thus began the big movement towards virginity, chastity, abstinence, celibacy. And The Devil, which is sexuality, Eve, the tempted and the temptress, and the Whore, who is the woman who has desire – Desire labelled as evil, driven underground, only to reappear in all the aspects of perversion and sadism, from the Inquisition onward. (Witness the attitudes towards that contemporary Whore, the Single Mother.) Pornography thrives, rape and child abuse go relatively unpunished. The sexual frustration of young men is used for every conceivable purpose, from selling cars to persuading them to become suicide bombers.

Let’s start again, with the mythical Mother Goddess. Let’s start with that mysterious force which broke the rule that everything tends to break down into simpler and simpler units – what was it, what force of creativity led matter to become more complex? Let us call that a female principle, and begin to experience that principle, which is expressed in the joy and delight connected to sexual, to reproductive behaviour, the profound happiness which can be found in loving connections to each other and to our children – it is all part of the same thing – treasuring life, treasuring all which conserves and continues life – making love is part of this flow, and as part of this flow, is a sacred act. Suppose we think of our bodies, of our reproductive functions, as sacred objects, sacred celebrations, to be approached with reverence and awe – for ourselves, for each other, for the act itself.

Of course I am mad, am I not. To think that, to begin to regard ourselves in this way, might transform almost everything about how our society operates.

We have suicide bombers, seduced by myths of 100 virgins waiting up in heaven. We have young men who never get drunk enough to dance. We have pornography, date rape, and experimenting with the new pornography – maybe torture is acceptable after all?

Do I have to go on?

Of course it is mad to think that all the craziness which is sending the world into ever more frightening currents and countercurrents of destruction might be underpinned by our conviction that sex is evil.

This couldn’t possibly be true.
